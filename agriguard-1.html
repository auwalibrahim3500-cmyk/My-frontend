Maize Leaf Blight

AI Confidence
96%
Maize · 2.3 hectares affected
⚠️ MODERATE
🦠 About This Disease
Northern Corn Leaf Blight (NCLB) caused by fungus Setosphaeria turcica. Long tan-grey lesions visible on leaves, 2.5–15cm long. Spreads rapidly in cool, humid conditions.
💊 Recommended Treatment
1
Apply Mancozeb fungicide at 2.5g/L water. Spray every 7–10 days.
2
Remove and destroy all infected plant debris from the field.
3
Ensure proper field drainage to reduce leaf wetness duration.
4
Re-scout after 2 weeks to monitor disease progression.
🛡️ Prevention Tips
✓
Use resistant hybrid seeds (e.g. SAMMAZ 14, DKC 8033)
✓
Practice crop rotation with legumes every 2 seasons
✓
Avoid overhead irrigation; use drip or furrow methods
✓
Monitor fields regularly — especially after heavy rainfall

Why are my maize leaves turning yellow?
🤖

🟡 Nitrogen Deficiency — Most common. Apply urea (46-0-0) at 100kg/ha along the base.

🍄 Northern Leaf Blight — Look for long greyish lesions. Use Mancozeb fungicide.

💧 Waterlogging — Check field drainage immediately.
